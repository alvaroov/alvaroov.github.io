Alvaro Ortiz-Vazquez was born in Mexico City, Mexico, but he immigrated to Minnesota in 1999 when he was four years old. Alvaro Graduated from Columbia University in the City of New York studying in 2016 with a bachelors degree in Computer-Science and Mathematics and a concentration in astronomy. He is also very interested by topics in astronomy and astrophysics including space travel, cosmology, and simulations. When not programming in Java or studying about active galactic nuclei, Alvaro is very passionate about working around issues of social justice and holds leadership positions in several activist student groups. He has been dedicated to organizing around human rights issues, and around raising awareness immigration issues in the US.  Alvaro has also been involved in several after-school tutoring programs since high school with the most recent being America Reads. Alvaro’s ideal career would merge the skills of computer programming and mathematics with a complex scientific application.
